John Ambrose Fleming

Sir John Ambrose Fleming was a british electrical engineer and physicist. He is famous for inventing the first vacuum tube and the left hand rule. John was the eldest of seven children, although he had none of his own over his lifetime. At a young age he was facinated with everything electronic, and at age eleven already had his own workshop and home made camera. Over his life he established himself as the leading expert in electric engineering. He died in 1945
